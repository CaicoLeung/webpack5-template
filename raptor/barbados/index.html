Barbados is close to British hearts founded by the friendliness of its people, mature infrastructure and some of the worldâ€™s finest dining, private villas, golfing, water sports and beaches; Caribbean lifestyle in Barbados is priceless.

The West Coast of Barbados spans from the most southern corner of the island, through Holetown to the most northern charming town of historic Speightstown. Properties along the west coast of the island are some of the most sought after in the Caribbean. The area has hosted royalty, presidents and prime ministers from the world over.

Options can include beachfront private villas and condominiums, colonial townhouse mews, exclusive gated golf and polo communities and modern hotel condominium developments. Designs vary from traditional elegance with contemporary accents to state-of-the-art modern smart homes and eco-friendly options with wrap around terraces of breath-taking views overlooking the Caribbean Sea.

The main Barbadian festival is the Crop Over, a traditional harvest festival that takes place over the summer to celebrate the local culture, music and food. There are many other popular festivals to choose from, such as the International Horticultural Flower Show, Reggae Music Festival, Holetown Festival, as well as The Barbados Food and Rum Festivals and the weekly local Oistins Fish Fry on the south coast.

Pamper yourself at world class spas and lounge at beach clubs such as Nikki Beach, with mega yachts nearby. Be adventurous on an island safari, cave excursions, submarine and catamaran tour. Choose from swimming with sea turtles, snorkelling, horseback riding on the beach, or simply take a stroll or sit back and relax in paradise. A wide variety of sporting activities are also available, including cricket matches, regattas, golf tournaments, polo season, or horse racing with the highly anticipated Sandy Lane Gold Cup. On the west coast of Barbados, you will find luxury shopping at its best in Holetown, with international brands in jewellery and fashion with local arts and crafts exhibits and house shops.
